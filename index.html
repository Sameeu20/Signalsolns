Digital Signal processing using TI processors

Our programmable digital signal processors (DSPs) operate in a variety of real-time signal processing applications, including audio and aerospace and defense. High volatge Magnetron Driver modules

High Voltage Magnetron Driver Modules are specialized components that convert low-voltage DC power into the high-voltage pulses needed to drive magnetrons. These modules are crucial in applications such as microwave ovens and radar systems.

Stm32 based projects

We provide Stm32 projects. As most modern electronics require more computing power, memory and a set of peripherals, 32-bit microcontrollers are growing in popularity among designers. One of the most commonly used 32-bit devices are the STM32 microcontrollers from ST Microelectronics, which are based on the successful ARM processors

Bare metals and build route programming

Bare metal programming includes everything from real-time operating systems and drivers to type 1 virtual machines. It's extremely fast and reliable because it doesn't have to go into different software abstraction layers, but it's also difficult to develop for a modern chip.

Split spectrum Processing (SSP)

Split spectrum processing is a frequency diversity technique used to improve the signal-to-noise ratio of ultrasound signals.

Li-ion Battery management systems

A battery management system (BMS) is a technology designed to oversee a battery pack, which is an assembly of battery cells, electrically organized in a row x column matrix configuration to enable the delivery of a targeted range of voltage and current over time against expected load scenarios.

Bidirectional DC-DC converter for lithium-ion battery

A bidirectional DC-DC converter is potentially used for power charging or discharging devices; however, the converter exhibits highly non-linear characteristics as its internal system parameters change depending on the operating mode and interference engagement. To cope with the nonlinearity, a bidirectional DC-DC converter based on time-delay control (TDC) is designed and verified for use with lithium-ion batteries.
